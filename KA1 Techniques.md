
The first Knowledge Area (KA 1) in the BABOK® Guide is **Business Analysis Planning and Monitoring**. This knowledge area encompasses five tasks: Plan Business Analysis Approach (3.1), Plan Stakeholder Engagement (3.2), Plan Business Analysis Governance (3.3), Plan Business Analysis Information Management (3.4), and Identify Business Analysis Performance Improvements (3.5).

Across these five tasks, business analysts utilize a wide variety of techniques to plan and organize their work. Here is the comprehensive list of techniques used during this knowledge area:

- **Brainstorming**: Used to identify possible activities, risks, stakeholder roles, approval names, and information management needs. Applied across all five tasks.
- **Business Cases**: Used to understand time-sensitivity, high-value elements, or uncertainty around a problem or opportunity (Task 3.1).
- **Business Rules Analysis**: Used to identify stakeholders who are the source of specific business rules (Task 3.2).
- **Document Analysis**: Used to review existing organizational assets, templates, and governance processes to aid in planning (Tasks 3.1, 3.2, 3.3).
- **Estimation**: Used to determine the time required to perform business analysis activities (Task 3.1).
- **Financial Analysis**: Used to assess how different approaches and delivery options affect the value delivered (Task 3.1).
- **Functional Decomposition**: Used to break down complex approaches and processes into more feasible components (Task 3.1).
- **Interviews**: Used to build plans, gain knowledge about stakeholder groups, uncover information needs, and evaluate performance with individuals or small groups. Applied across all five tasks.
- **Item Tracking**: Used to track issues, risks, or conflicts raised by stakeholders during planning, governance, and performance assessments. Applied across the knowledge area.
- **Lessons Learned**: Used to identify previous experiences (successes and challenges) with planning and governance to improve current and future work. Applied across all five tasks.
- **Metrics and Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**: Used to determine what metrics are appropriate for assessing and tracking business analysis performance (Task 3.5).
- **Mind Mapping**: Used to identify potential stakeholders, understand their relationships, and categorize the kinds of information that must be managed (Tasks 3.2, 3.4).
- **Observation**: Used to witness and evaluate business analysis performance firsthand (Task 3.5).
- **Organizational Modelling**: Used to determine unique stakeholder needs and understand roles/responsibilities to define an effective governance approach (Tasks 3.2, 3.3).
- **Process Analysis**: Used to analyze existing business analysis processes to identify opportunities for improvement (Task 3.5).
- **Process Modelling**: Used to define, document, and categorize the business analysis processes, governance methods, and information management steps. Applied across all five tasks.
- **Reviews**: Used to validate and gather feedback on the selected business analysis approaches, governance plans, and performance improvement deliverables (Tasks 3.1, 3.3, 3.5).
- **Risk Analysis and Management**: Used to assess risks for selecting an approach, managing stakeholder attitudes, and identifying events that may impact business analysis performance (Tasks 3.1, 3.2, 3.5).
- **Root Cause Analysis**: Used to identify the underlying causes of failures or difficulties in accomplishing business analysis work (Task 3.5).
- **Scope Modelling**: Used to determine the boundaries of the solution as an input to planning and to show stakeholders who interact with the solution from outside its scope (Tasks 3.1, 3.2).
- **Stakeholder List, Map, or Personas**: Used to depict the relationship of stakeholders to the solution and to one another (Task 3.2).
- **Survey or Questionnaire**: Used to identify activities and shared characteristics, solicit input on information management, and gather feedback on stakeholder satisfaction. Applied across all five tasks.
- **Workshops**: Used to build plans, interact with stakeholder groups, determine governance participants, and generate performance improvement ideas in a team setting. Applied across all five tasks
